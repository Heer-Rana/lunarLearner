Stars

Overview

Stars are massive celestial bodies composed primarily of hydrogen and helium that produce light and heat from the nuclear fusion occurring at their cores.

Star Life Cycle

Main Sequence Stars

Most stars spend the majority of their lives in the main sequence phase, fusing hydrogen into helium in their cores.

Red Giants

When stars exhaust their hydrogen fuel, they expand into red giants.

Final Stages

Depending on their mass, stars end their lives as white dwarfs, neutron stars, or black holes.

Types of Stars

Red Dwarfs

The most common type of star in the universe.

Yellow Dwarfs

Medium-sized stars like our Sun.

Blue Giants

The largest and hottest stars.
